Common Questions

Frequently Asked Questions

Do I need a referral to see a chiropractor?
No referral is needed. You can contact us directly to schedule a new patient appointment. In most states, chiropractic care is considered primary care and you can self-refer at any time.
Does chiropractic care hurt?
Most patients experience relief immediately following an adjustment. Some may notice mild soreness for 24–48 hours, similar to the feeling after exercise. Our doctors adjust their technique based on your comfort level.
What should I bring to my first appointment?
Please bring a photo ID, your insurance card, any relevant X-rays or MRI results, and a list of current medications. Arrive 10 minutes early to complete your new patient paperwork — or download our forms in advance from this website.
How many visits will I need?
Every patient is different. After a comprehensive evaluation on your first visit, we'll give you a realistic treatment plan with estimated visit frequency and duration. Many acute cases resolve in 6–12 visits; chronic conditions may require longer-term care.
Do you accept my insurance?
We accept most major medical insurance plans, auto insurance (PIP/MedPay), and workers' compensation. Call our office and we'll verify your benefits at no charge before your first appointment.
Is chiropractic care safe during pregnancy?
Yes — prenatal chiropractic care is safe and highly effective for relieving back pain, sciatica, and pelvic discomfort during pregnancy. Our doctors are certified in the Webster Technique, a specific prenatal chiropractic protocol.
